Education at risk says Georgia's teachers as they join protests

Politics

Teachers in Georgia are protesting against the government's anti-European policies, warning that education is at risk.
Over 2,500 educators have signed a petition, asserting that the current regime makes it impossible to perform their jobs effectively.
They call for a return to Georgia's European path, emphasizing the importance of education for democracy and a pro-European future.
On December 2nd, schools, universities, and kindergartens will boycott classes in a coordinated strike against these policies.
The protest aims to secure a democratic future for children, asserting that they deserve an education free from oligarchic and foreign influence.
Parents and teachers are united, voicing concerns over their children's futures, particularly after the disruptions caused by the pandemic.
The stakes are high, as they rally to remind officials that their primary duty is to serve the interests of the people, not oligarchs or external pressures.
This movement is growing rapidly, with no signs of abating.
The urgent call for change underscores the necessity of safeguarding Georgia's European aspirations for the next generation.
