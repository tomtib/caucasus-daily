Caucasian journal warns of elites abandoning Georgian government

Politics

Georgia's political landscape is shifting as key institutions and businesses increasingly oppose the ruling regime, according to Caucasian Journal.
President Salome Zurabishvili has claimed sole legitimacy in light of the controversial decision to postpone EU accession talks until 2028.
Prominent figures in the banking sector, notably the Bank of Georgia and TBC Bank, have publicly condemned the government's stance, emphasizing the urgency of Euro-integration.
Telecommunications leaders, Magti and Silknet, have also criticized the government, pointing to the need for maintaining constitutional order and reaffirming commitment to European values.
In government institutions, protests by Foreign Ministry employees and the National Bank of Georgia's staff highlight opposition, with many resignations occurring in protest of the government's decisions.
Local government workers in Tbilisi and Batumi have distanced themselves from these actions, echoing the public's demand for continued EU integration.
With rising dissent among elites and the public, Georgia may face significant political changes ahead as the push for new elections intensifies.
